## Sub-Problems

At each iteration of the Dantzig-Wolfe algorithm, the <TermLink term="pricing sub-problem" />s
determine whether any new column can improve the current master solution.

### Pricing Sub-Problem Formulation

Let $\pi \in \mathbb{R}^m$ be the <TermLink term="dual variables" /> of the coupling constraints
and $\sigma_i \in \mathbb{R}$ be the dual of the convexity constraint for sub-problem $i$.

The i-th pricing sub-problem is:

<MathBlock
  formula="\min_{x_i \in P_i} \; (c_i - (A_0^i)^T \pi)^T x_i - \sigma_i"
  label="Pricing sub-problem i"
/>

The expression $(c_i - (A_0^i)^T \pi)^T x_i^* - \sigma_i$ is the <TermLink term="reduced cost" />
of the column corresponding to extreme point $x_i^*$.

### The Bounded Case

If the sub-problem is **bounded** (i.e., $P_i$ is bounded or the objective is bounded below on
$P_i$), then the optimal solution is an extreme point $v_{ij}^*$. If its reduced cost is
**negative**, it becomes a candidate column for the master problem:

<MathBlock
  formula="\bar{c}_{ij} = (c_i^T - \pi^T A_0^i) v_{ij}^* - \sigma_i < 0 \implies \text{enter column}"
  label="Entering condition"
/>

### The Unbounded Case

If the sub-problem is **unbounded** (the minimisation has no lower bound), then the optimal
direction is an extreme ray $r_{ik}$ along which the objective decreases without bound. This
extreme ray becomes an entering column for the master problem:

<MathBlock
  formula="(c_i^T - \pi^T A_0^i) r_{ik} < 0 \implies \text{the original LP is unbounded}"
  label="Unbounded direction"
/>

<Callout variant="warning" title="Unbounded Sub-Problem">
  An unbounded sub-problem indicates that the original LP is itself **unbounded**. The algorithm
  terminates immediately with this certificate.
</Callout>

### Infeasibility Detection

If the master RMP is infeasible (e.g., the initial basis has no feasible solution), a Phase I
approach or big-M method must be used first. Sub-problem infeasibility cannot occur because each
$P_i$ is assumed non-empty by the problem assumptions.
