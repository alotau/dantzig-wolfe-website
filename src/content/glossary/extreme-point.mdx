---
term: extreme point
shortDef: >
  A vertex of a convex feasible region that cannot be expressed as a convex
  combination of two other distinct feasible points; the LP optimal always lies
  at an extreme point (if one exists).
relatedTerms:
  - convexity constraint
  - master problem
  - column generation
---

A point $v \in \mathcal{X}$ is an **extreme point** (vertex) of the convex set
$\mathcal{X}$ if there do not exist distinct points $v^1, v^2 \in \mathcal{X}$
and $\theta \in (0,1)$ such that $v = \theta v^1 + (1-\theta) v^2$.

The **fundamental theorem of linear programming** states that if an LP has an
optimal solution, it has one at an extreme point of the feasible polyhedron.

Dantzig-Wolfe Decomposition exploits this: the master problem selects a convex
combination of sub-problem extreme points, and column generation searches for the
extreme point with the most negative reduced cost (most improving direction).
