---
term: block-angular LP
shortDef: >
  A linear programme whose constraint matrix has a staircase or block-diagonal
  structure with a set of linking (coupling) rows that connect otherwise
  independent sub-matrices.
relatedTerms:
  - coupling constraints
  - Dantzig-Wolfe Decomposition
  - pricing sub-problem
---

A **block-angular linear programme** has a coefficient matrix of the form

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
A_0 \\
A_1 &        \\
    & A_2    \\
    &        & \ddots \\
    &        &        & A_K
\end{pmatrix}
$$

where $A_0$ contains the _coupling constraints_ shared by all blocks, and each
$A_k$ ($k = 1, \ldots, K$) is the constraint matrix of an otherwise independent
sub-problem. This structure arises naturally in multi-commodity flow, production
planning, and cutting-stock problems.
