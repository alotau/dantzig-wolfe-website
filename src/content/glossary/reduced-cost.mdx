---
term: reduced cost
shortDef: >
  The net change in the objective function per unit increase of a non-basic
  variable, accounting for the shadow prices (dual variables) of the constraints
  it participates in. A negative reduced cost indicates a potentially improving column.
relatedTerms:
  - dual variables
  - column generation
  - pricing sub-problem
---

For a column $x_k$ with objective coefficient $c_k$ and constraint coefficient
$A_k$ in the master problem, the **reduced cost** is:

$$
\bar{c}_k = c_k - \pi^{\top} A_k
$$

where $\pi$ are the dual variables of the current restricted master problem.

- If $\bar{c}_k < 0$: introducing $x_k$ decreases the objective → add the column.
- If $\bar{c}_k \geq 0$ for all columns: the current solution is LP-optimal.
