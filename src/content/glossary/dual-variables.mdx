---
term: dual variables
shortDef: >
  The shadow prices associated with each constraint of a linear programme; in
  Dantzig-Wolfe Decomposition the master problem's dual variables act as price
  signals that guide each sub-problem to generate improving columns.
relatedTerms:
  - reduced cost
  - coupling constraints
  - pricing sub-problem
---

For an LP $\min c^{\top}x$ subject to $Ax \leq b$, $x \geq 0$, the **dual
variables** (or shadow prices) $\pi \geq 0$ satisfy the dual LP:

$$
\max_{\pi \geq 0} \; b^{\top} \pi \quad \text{s.t.} \quad A^{\top} \pi \leq c.
$$

In Dantzig-Wolfe Decomposition the dual variables of the restricted master
problem's coupling constraints are passed to each pricing sub-problem, where
they reduce the effective objective coefficient of each sub-problem's variables,
allowing the sub-problem to find columns that improve the master objective.
